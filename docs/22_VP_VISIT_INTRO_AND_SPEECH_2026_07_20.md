## 30-Second Introduction

Hi, I’m Milton Torres, a Mitacs Globalink Research Intern from Mexico, working with Dr. Rita Orji, Dr. Fidelia Orji, and Dr. Grace Ataguba. My project, FitQuest, combines camera-based pose and object detection with an ESP32 and IMU, plus Garmin heart-rate context, to control a simple web-based fitness game. The goal is to create reliable movement and exertion signals that can also support future adaptive and rehabilitation systems.

## If They Ask What You Are Working on Now

I have completed the 3D model of the ESP32/IMU case, and my next step is to print and validate it. After that, I will connect the normalized signals to a simple web-based game, run integrated motion tests, and organize the evidence for the final paper.

## If They Ask About the Difference Between IronQuest 3D and FitQuest

The original project was proposed as IronQuest 3D, a smart-dumbbell fitness gaming platform with a 3D web-based game. After arriving, we narrowed the immediate research deliverable to the sensor-fusion middleware that makes that platform possible: reliable camera, IMU, and wearable signals in a normalized format. FitQuest is the current working presentation of that sensing foundation. We are still delivering the game as a small web-based proof of concept, rather than attempting a complex RPG or a complete commercial platform.

## Technical Update Speech — Approximately 45 Seconds

Last week I completed the 3D model of the protective case for the ESP32 and BNO08x IMU. The goal is to move from a fragile breadboard setup to a repeatable physical prototype that keeps the electronics and sensor orientation stable during movement testing. The model is complete, but the first print is still pending. This week I will print it, validate the fit and cable access, and test its stability during motion. Then I will run a short integrated session with the camera and IMU, use Garmin heart-rate data as supplementary physiological context, and connect the signals to a simple web-based game. The result should be a more reliable setup, a concrete end-to-end demonstration, and stronger evidence for the sensor-fusion pipeline and final paper.
